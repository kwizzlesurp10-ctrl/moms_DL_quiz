Study Guide

Read these key facts carefully. They're the most important things on the test!

Road Signs & Markings

  • Red signs = Stop or do not do something
  • Yellow / Yellow-Green = Warning (school zones, curves, pedestrians)
  • Orange = Construction / Work zone — slow down!
  • Broken white line = You can change lanes or pass
  • Solid yellow line = No passing (double solid = very dangerous)
  • Red arrow = Do NOT turn in that direction until green

Right-of-Way Rules

  • At 4-way stop or no signs: Yield to the driver on your right
  • Always yield to pedestrians in crosswalks (even unmarked)
  • School bus with red lights + stop arm = STOP 20+ feet away. Do not pass!
  • Emergency vehicles (lights + siren) = Pull to the right and stop
  • Move Over Law: Slow down + change lanes away from stopped emergency/tow/maintenance vehicles
  • Roundabouts: Yield to traffic already in the circle

Speed & Passing

  • Default limits: 30 mph in towns, 55 mph on highways, 10-15 mph in alleys
  • Always drive at a speed that is reasonable for conditions (weather, traffic)
  • Pass on the left. Return to right lane when you can see the other car in your mirror
  • Never pass on hills, curves, or within 100 ft of intersections or railroad crossings
  • Speeding 20+ mph over = bigger fines. 100+ mph = license revoked 6 months

Winter & Night Driving (Very Important in MN!)

  • Headlights ON when visibility is less than 500 feet OR in rain, snow, sleet, hail, or fog
  • Use low beams in snow, fog, or when following another car
  • Clear ALL snow and ice from windows, mirrors, and lights before driving
  • Increase following distance to 4–5 seconds in bad weather
  • If stranded: Stay in car, hazards on, run heater 10 min per hour, keep a window cracked
  • Never pass a snowplow — wait until you can see the whole vehicle

Distracted Driving & Seat Belts

  • Hands-free law: You may NOT hold your phone while driving — even at a red light! Use voice commands or a mounted phone only.
  • Seat belts are PRIMARY law — police can stop you just for not wearing one. Everyone in every seat must be buckled!
  • Never text, scroll, or use social media while driving
  • Child seats: Follow the law exactly — rear-facing until at least age 2, etc.

Alcohol, Drugs & Other Rules

  • Legal limit: 0.08% BAC (0.04% for commercial). Even less can impair you!
  • Never drive after drinking or taking medications that make you drowsy
  • Right turn on red: OK after full stop + yield, unless sign says no
  • Never block intersections — wait until you can clear it completely
  • Railroad crossings: Stop if lights flashing or gates down. Look both ways!

Pro Tip from the Manual

Use the SIPDE method every time you drive: Scan, Identify, Predict, Decide, Execute. It saves lives!

Ohio vs Minnesota

Important things that are DIFFERENT here in Minnesota

Seat Belt Law

Minnesota (PRIMARY): Police can pull you over just for not wearing your seat belt. Every person in every seat must be buckled up. Fine: $25.

Ohio (SECONDARY): They can only ticket you if they stopped you for something else first. Usually only front seat required in many cases.

Cell Phone / Hands-Free Law

Minnesota: You CANNOT hold your phone at all while driving — even when stopped at a red light or in traffic. Must be mounted or use voice commands / single touch only. Fines start at $100+.

Ohio: Texting is banned, but holding the phone for calls may be allowed in some situations (check current Ohio law). MN is stricter.

Winter Driving

Minnesota: The test has many questions about snow, ice, black ice, snowplows, and what to do if stranded. You must clear every bit of snow/ice. Headlights required in snow/rain/fog.

Ohio: Winters are milder. Less emphasis on these topics on the Ohio test.

Other Big Differences

  • Move Over Law is very strictly enforced in MN — slow down AND move over for any stopped emergency, tow, or road work vehicle with lights.
  • School bus rules are the same, but MN has specific rules about divided highways (you don't stop if bus is on opposite side of divided road).
  • Headlights: Must be on whenever you can't see 500 feet ahead or in rain/snow/fog.
  • MN has reduced conflict intersections and J-turns that you won't see as much in Ohio.
  • Default alley speed is often 10-15 mph in MN.
